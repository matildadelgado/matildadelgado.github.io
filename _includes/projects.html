Anomalies and Bordisms of Non-supersymmetric Strings

In this work, we analyze global anomalies in the three known tachyon-free, non-supersymmetric string theories in ten dimensions by computing the relevant twisted string bordism groups. We show that two of these theories are free of global anomalies, while the third exhibits only limited obstructions, and we further compute lower-dimensional bordism groups that detect extended objects in these theories.

Dualities and the Compactifiability of Moduli Spaces

We argue that dualities in string theory impose strong constraints on the low-energy effective action, requiring the moduli space of massless fields to be compactifiable, in the sense that its volume grows no faster than that of Euclidean space. We propose that this compactifiability follows from a finiteness principle in quantum gravity, and further show that it implies the lattice of charged objects transforms in a semisimple representation of the duality group, as illustrated by numerous string theory examples.

Dynamical Aspects of Cobordisms

The Swampland cobordism conjecture predicts the existence of new defects and topology-changing transitions in quantum gravity; however, it does not specify the underlying dynamics. We investigated the dynamical mechanisms governing the topology-changing transitions implied by the cobordism conjecture.
