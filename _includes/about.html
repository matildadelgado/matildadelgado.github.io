About Me

I am a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard University and MPP Munich.

I develop theoretical tools to uncover the structure of quantum gravity and its low-energy signatures, particularly beyond supersymmetric settings. My work draws on string theory and general consistency principles, including ideas from the swampland program, to determine which effective field theories and physical objects can arise from a consistent theory of (non-supersymmetric) quantum gravity.

My research aims to chart the non-supersymmetric landscape and to provide sharp tests of swampland conjectures beyond the regime of supersymmetry.



Education

I performed my doctoral studies at the Institute of Theoretical Physics in Madrid (IFT UAM-CSIC). Before that, I studied high energy physics at Institut Polytechnique de Paris and ETH Zürich.
